Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

I used to work in a team of three, on a rotating shift to cover 24 hours a day, 5 days a week. We required 2 people minimum per shift to keep prodution running, so if people were away we would cover for four hours either side of our shift depending on which was required. I was covering for someone, working on after my own shift. My team leader at the time encouraged collaboration, if you saw something that needed doing, you should do it. He hated the "that's not my job" mentality. So, while working on the mezzanine floor, I noticed a tank was running empty and required filling to complete the discharge process. So I filled it and completed the paperwork when returning to the main deck, saving the other operato a trip up two flights of stairs. Pretty standard practice on my own shift. When the operator who I was working with returned and saw that I had done said task, he confronted me, aggresively. I truly believed his outrage was faked and he was winding me up, the reaction was way over the top, so I laughed uncomfortably. Which was a big mistake, there was a lot of swearing! Long story short, I bit my tongue finished out the shift and escalated the incident to managment. In hindsight, I could have simply asked his expectations at the start of the shift, it was his shift to run (however badly) and I should not have made assumptions.
